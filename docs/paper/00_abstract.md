# The Predictive Universe

**Abstract**

This paper proposes the Predictive Universe (PU) framework, a first-principles model that derives constraints on effective physical structure from the operational logic of interacting predictive systems. Rather than assuming a pre-existing material world, the framework takes the *Cogito*—the self-verifying certainty of ongoing awareness—as its epistemic foundation. On operational grounds (Appendix P), this awareness is characterized as fundamentally predictive: a process that must model its environment to persist. Physical structure then emerges from the *Prediction Optimization Problem* (POP), the resource-constrained imperative to maximize predictive accuracy, governed by the *Principle of Compression Efficiency* (PCE) which selects dynamics that optimize the trade-off between predictive fidelity and resource cost. The *Principle of Physical Instantiation* (PPI) bridges epistemology and physics: when predictive processes operate under finite resources and over finite time, their operational constraints determine the form of effective physical law. All results labeled Theorem/Proposition/Lemma are internal consequences of the POP/PCE formalism. In particular, Appendix G proves that whenever the ordered inactive-sector $(1,2,3)$ decomposition is required as global smooth bundle data, its realization factors through the minimal flag lift $\widetilde X = \mathrm{Flag}_{1,2,3}(Q)$ over $\mathrm{Gr}(2,8)$ in the universal sense of Theorem G.8.4e.1. Empirical content enters only through the explicit PPI mapping contract that identifies PU invariants with standard observables.

The framework derives—within the formal model defined by POP/PCE and its physical-instantiation mapping (PPI)—a chain of constraints that reproduces several structural features of known physics. A fundamental logical limit on self-prediction, the **Self-Referential Paradox of Accurate Prediction** (SPAP), motivates a probabilistic effective description of MPU dynamics; under the non-contextual additivity and refinement-continuity constraints selected by PCE (Theorem G.1.7; Appendix G.1), the probability rule is fixed to the Born form. The framework's fundamental constituents, **Minimal Predictive Units** (MPUs), are characterized by the **Horizon Constant** $K_0 = 3$ bits—the minimum complexity for SPAP encodability—which imposes the lower bound $d_0 \ge 2^{K_0} = 8$ on the MPU Hilbert space; PCE selects the minimal saturating case $d_0 = 8$ at the PCE-Attractor. The logically necessary 2-to-1 state merge in SPAP implies a Landauer entropy-production bound $\varepsilon \ge \ln 2$ per predictive cycle; at the PCE-Attractor this bound saturates, $\varepsilon = \ln 2$, and combined with the PPI this yields the active kernel dimension $a = 2$ and interface mode count $M = 2a(d_0 - a) = 24$. Under the mode–channel correspondence that identifies the $M = 24$ interface modes of the PCE-Attractor with the kissing number $K(D)$ of the $D$-dimensional Euclideanized local tangent space (Appendix Z, Theorem Z.11), the constraint $M = K(D)$ selects $D = 4$, corresponding to $3+1$ macroscopic dimensions. $$\text{Cogito} \xrightarrow{} \text{Prediction} \xrightarrow{} \text{SPAP} + \text{Landauer} \xrightarrow{} \varepsilon \ge \ln 2 \xrightarrow{\text{PCE}} \varepsilon = \ln 2 \xrightarrow{} a = 2 \xrightarrow{} M = 24 \xrightarrow{} D = 4$$

The continuum layer is operational rather than ontological: the real world does not have to become an actual continuum. It only has to generate continuum behavior as a finite-resolution effective closure. Exact real-number continua, exact uniformly refinable Lie symmetries, UV infinities, curvature singularities, and globally completed branch ontologies are treated as mathematical completions whose physical content is limited by finite MPU distinguishability, finite channel capacity, and irreducible update cost.
